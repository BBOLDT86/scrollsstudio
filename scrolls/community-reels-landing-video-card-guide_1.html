Two HTML files work together — a Satellite Recorder (phones) and a Master Card Builder (laptop). Participants record full HD video on their phones, send the file back, and the coordinator loads them all on a laptop to build the final card.

File Size Warning

Each satellite file can be 30–90 MB. With 8 people, the final card could be 500+ MB. This is too large for iMessage (which caps around 100 MB). Use AirDrop, Google Drive, Dropbox, or email to send the final assembled card to the recipient. Individual satellites can be sent back via AirDrop or cloud link — iMessage may compress or reject them above ~100 MB.

Step-by-Step Flow

👤

Coordinator: Text the recorder

Send satellite-recorder.html to the group chat. Tell everyone: "Open this, record a video for [Name], then send the file back to me."

🎬

Each person: Record on your phone

Open the satellite file. Enter your name. Tap record. Speak your message (up to 90 seconds). Tap stop. Review it. Tap "Save & Share" — it creates a file named after you. Send it back via AirDrop (fastest) or save to Files and share via cloud link.

📥

Coordinator: Collect on your laptop

Gather all satellite HTML files into a folder on your laptop. AirDrop directly to your Mac, or download from shared cloud links. You'll have: Sarah.html, Mike.html, etc.

💻

Coordinator: Build on your laptop

Open master-card.html in Chrome or Safari on your laptop. Set the recipient name, occasion, and theme. Then drag-and-drop all the satellite files onto the drop zone (or use the file picker). The master reads each one and shows them in a grid.

👁️

Coordinator: Preview

Click "Preview Card" to see exactly what the recipient will see. Click each video tile to play. Go back and adjust if needed — reorder, remove, or add more files.

🎁

Coordinator: Export & send

Click "Export Final Card." It downloads a single self-contained HTML file. Send this to the recipient via AirDrop, Google Drive link, Dropbox, or email attachment. They open it in any browser — works forever, no internet needed.

Sending Large Files

Since files are 30–90 MB each (and the final card can be hundreds of MB), here are the best transfer methods:

AirDrop (Best for Apple devices)

Instant, no size limits, no compression. Works phone→laptop and laptop→phone. The ideal way for participants to return satellite files and for you to send the final card.

Cloud Links (Universal)

Upload to Google Drive, iCloud Drive, or Dropbox. Share a link. Works across all devices and platforms. Best option if any participants are on Android.

Email (Up to ~25 MB)

Works for shorter clips (~15–20 seconds). Most email providers cap attachments at 25 MB, but Gmail/Outlook auto-convert to a cloud link for larger files.

iMessage Limits

iMessage works fine for sending the small satellite-recorder.html file (~30 KB) to the group. But the returned satellite files (30–90 MB each) and the final card may exceed iMessage's ~100 MB limit. Use AirDrop or cloud links for those.

Technical Notes

Video encoding: The recorder captures 1080p at 8 Mbps video / 128 kbps audio using the phone's native MediaRecorder codec (H.264 on iPhone, VP9 on most Android). Video is base64-encoded and embedded directly inside the HTML file.

One name, one clip: The master deduplicates by name. If someone sends two versions, loading the newer file replaces the older one.

Self-contained: Every file — satellite and final card — is a single HTML file with all video data embedded. No internet required to view. No links to expire. Works on any device with a browser.

Privacy: Nothing is uploaded to any server. All processing happens on-device. Videos only exist inside the HTML files on the participants' devices and wherever you choose to store/send them.

Browser requirements: The recorder needs a modern mobile browser (Safari 14+, Chrome 80+). The master works best in Chrome, Safari, Edge, or Firefox on a laptop. The final card plays in any modern browser.

Common Questions

Can I re-record if I don't like my take?
Yes. Hit "Re-record" in the satellite. It replaces your previous clip. Only the latest file you send back counts.
How long can my clip be?
Up to 90 seconds. A 90-second clip at full quality will be roughly 70–90 MB. Shorter clips (15–30s) will be 15–30 MB and easier to transfer.
Does this work on Android?
The recorder works on Android Chrome. The sharing flow differs — use your cloud app or messaging app instead of AirDrop. The master and final card work the same on any device.
Why does the master need a laptop?
Loading multiple 30–90 MB files in a mobile browser can cause crashes or extreme slowness. A laptop has the memory and processing power to handle hundreds of MB of embedded video comfortably. The drag-and-drop interface is also much easier with a trackpad/mouse.
Can the recipient see who contributed?
Yes. Each video shows as a tile with the person's name and optional written message. They tap/click to play each one.
What if someone's file is corrupted?
The master will skip unreadable files and warn you. Have that person re-record and re-send.
What's the max number of people?
Practically 8–10 at full 90-second clips (the final card would be ~700 MB). With shorter 15–30 second clips, you could fit 15–20 people easily. The laptop browser's memory is the bottleneck — 8 GB RAM laptops can handle about 1 GB of embedded video.
