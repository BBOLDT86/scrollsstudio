The thesis
You remember when
friendship meant knocking
on someone's door.
When Saturday mornings were made for cartoons, not scrolling feeds. When happiness came from mixtapes, bike rides, and street lights telling you it was time to go home.
But here's what makes your generation different. You were the Bridge. The last to know life without technology. And the first to grow up adapting to it.
You lived through payphones and dial-up, and then watched the world transform into something faster, louder, and harder to escape.
Maybe that's why people born in the '80s and '90s carry a certain ache. A nostalgia for simplicity. A quiet wish that joy could still feel as easy as it once did.
You carry two worlds
inside you.

We're building where those worlds meet.

The product
We're not building
another platform that
demands your attention.

We're building quiet tools that work in a single file. No accounts. No subscriptions. No tracking. No feed. Just the thing you needed, ready when you open it.

A recipe card that feels like your mom's kitchen. A travel planner you send like a mixtape. A video call that just works, the way picking up the phone used to.

Technology got complicated because it was built to keep you. We're building technology designed to let you go.

We call them
Scrolls.

A single document that contains everything — the tool, the data, the design. One file. No server. No account. Just open it.
