01The Pitch

You already know what you need. You just can't afford to build it.

Every organization knows exactly which tools they wish they had. A video library like YouTube — but for internal training. A messaging platform like WhatsApp — but encrypted and on your own infrastructure. A CRM like Salesforce — but designed for your actual workflow. A project board like Jira — but without the $8/seat/month tax.

The problem has never been imagination. It's been implementation. Custom software costs six figures, takes 12+ months, and requires developers, servers, and ongoing maintenance. So you settle for generic SaaS tools that don't quite fit, pay per-seat fees that never stop growing, and hand your data to vendors whose interests don't align with yours.

DocChain eliminates the gap between what you want and what you can actually deploy. Every application on this list exists as a self-contained HTML file — roughly 40 kilobytes — running entirely in the browser. No server. No database. No subscription. No developer required. Peer-to-peer sync, end-to-end encryption, and a blockchain-anchored audit trail are built into the protocol layer so every app inherits them automatically.

03Communicate

Your team needs to talk. Not through someone else's servers.

Every message your team sends through Slack, Teams, WhatsApp, or email passes through a vendor's infrastructure. They can read it. They can store it. They can be compelled to hand it over. DocChain messaging is end-to-end encrypted and peer-to-peer — your conversations never exist on any server at any point.

Team messaging that belongs to your team

Communication
Replaces → Slack · Discord · MS Teams · WhatsApp · Telegram · Zoom

Channels, threads, direct messages, file sharing, and video links — the same collaboration patterns your team already uses, but running locally. A new hire doesn't need a license. They open the HTML file and they're connected. When your field team is at a plant with no internet, messages queue locally and sync the moment they're back online. No "unable to connect" errors. No messages lost in the void.

Email without the surveillance infrastructure

Email
Replaces → Gmail · Outlook

Compose, thread, search, label, filter — the complete email workflow running as a peer-to-peer protocol. No mail server to configure or maintain. Messages are encrypted end-to-end by default. No vendor scanning your correspondence to sell you ads or comply with blanket government requests.

Video and screen sharing without the meeting tax

Video
Replaces → Zoom · Loom

WebRTC already powers browser-based video — DocChain leverages the same protocol for direct peer-to-peer video calls with no relay server. Record async video walkthroughs Loom-style and share them as encrypted files within the peer network. No 40-minute free tier limit. No recording stored on a vendor's cloud.

09Protect

Passwords, credentials, and secrets — under your control.

Every password manager is a bet that the vendor's security is better than yours. LastPass proved that bet can go wrong. DocChain's security tools keep your most sensitive data encrypted locally with keys that never leave your devices.

Password and credential management

Security
Replaces → 1Password · LastPass

AES-GCM encrypted vault with PBKDF2 key derivation, random salts, and deliberately expensive hashing to resist brute force. Your credentials are encrypted at rest on your device. Share vault entries with team members via the peer-to-peer protocol — each entry re-wrapped with the recipient's public key. Revoke access by rotating keys. No cloud vault to breach. No vendor to trust with the keys to your kingdom.

The Security Architecture

Every DocChain application inherits the same security stack: AES-GCM encryption at rest, WebRTC end-to-end encryption in transit, capability-based access control, and blockchain-anchored audit trails. This isn't a feature bolted onto each app — it's the foundation the protocol provides. A password manager, a CRM, and a spreadsheet all get the same military-grade encryption automatically.

12How It Works

The technology that makes 100 apps in 100 files possible

You don't need to understand the engineering to use DocChain — just like you don't need to understand TCP/IP to use the internet. But for those who want to know why this works, here's the architecture in plain language:

CRDTs

Conflict-Free Data Types

The mathematical guarantee that concurrent edits merge correctly without a server. Two people edit a spreadsheet at the same time? Both changes survive. Always.

WebRTC

Peer-to-Peer Sync

Browsers connect directly. Your data goes from your device to your teammate's device with nothing in between. Encrypted the entire way.

Blockchain

Tamper-Proof Audit Trail

Periodic snapshots of every document's state are anchored to a public ledger. Nobody — not even an admin — can rewrite history.

HTML

Self-Contained Apps

Each application is a single HTML file. Complete rendering engine. No server. No build step. No dependency. Open it in a browser and it works.

Why This Matters

These aren't toy demos. The document editor is a full rich-text environment in ~1,000 lines of code. The spreadsheet supports formulas, multi-sheet tabs, sorting, and CSV export. The encryption uses AES-GCM with PBKDF2 key derivation — the same primitives your bank uses. A single operation is ~120 bytes. A very active document generates ~1.2MB/day of operation log. Monthly compaction keeps everything manageable even on a phone.

13Getting Started

Start with one app. Add the rest when you're ready.

You don't have to deploy 100 applications on day one. Pick the one tool that costs you the most pain or the most money. Replace that first. See how it feels. Then add the next one. There is no migration commitment, no all-or-nothing cutover, no rip-and-replace.

The Typical Path

Week 1: Replace your most expensive or most painful SaaS tool with its DocChain equivalent. CRM, project board, document editor — whatever hurts the most.

Month 1: Add 2–3 more tools. Team messaging, spreadsheets, and time tracking are common early additions because they're used daily by everyone.

Month 3: Departmental tools — engineering gets spec review and drawing markup, sales gets the quote generator and intel dashboards, accounting gets expense reports.

Month 6: The full suite is deployed. Your SaaS bill is a fraction of what it was. Your data is on your devices. Your tools work offline. And you own everything.
