ZEN ELITE FEI DRESSAGE WORLD CUP™ FINAL 2026

The World's Finest Dressage Horses and Riders Descend on Fort Worth

Defending champion Charlotte Fry and Glamourdale lead a star-studded international field to Dickies Arena as Texas hosts its first-ever Dressage World Cup Final
The Horselovers Concierge · Dressage Desk

FORT WORTH, TEXAS — For the first time in its 40-year history, the FEI Dressage World Cup Final will be held in the Lone Star State when the Zen Elite FEI Dressage World Cup™ Final opens at Dickies Arena on April 8, 2026. The five-day championship, organized by Split Rock Jumping Tour in partnership with the City of Fort Worth and the Fort Worth Sports Commission, is expected to draw more than 60,000 spectators and generate an economic impact exceeding $21 million for the region.

The event marks the series' return to American soil for the first time since Omaha in 2023. Dickies Arena, the state-of-the-art 14,000-seat multipurpose venue located adjacent to the historic Will Rogers Memorial Center, will be transformed into a world-class equestrian theater complete with premium footing, international broadcast infrastructure, and a vendor village featuring top equestrian brands including CWD, Devoucoux, Only Fools and Horses, Tack N Rider, LM Boots, Ariat, and dozens more.

All eyes will be on Britain's Charlotte Fry and her legendary black stallion Glamourdale, the reigning World Cup champions fresh off their dominant title run at the 2025 Final in Basel, Switzerland. The pair have been virtually untouchable this indoor season, winning World Cup qualifiers in London and Amsterdam with Freestyle scores approaching 90%. Fry, who sits atop the FEI Dressage World Rankings alongside Glamourdale, is automatically qualified as defending champion.

"I trust Glammie completely, and if the crowd gets loud, he has no problem walking into an arena like this. He loves it."
— Charlotte Fry, Defending World Cup Champion

Chasing her will be Germany's Isabell Werth — widely regarded as the greatest dressage rider of all time — with her rising star Wendy de Fontaine, plus Britain's Becky Moody and the crowd-favorite Jagerbomb, Denmark's Cathrine Laudrup-Dufour, Sweden's Patrik Kittel, and a host of international contenders from across four continental leagues. The top nine athletes from the Western European League, plus representatives from the North American, Central European, and Pacific leagues, will converge for the Grand Prix on Thursday evening and the climactic Grand Prix Freestyle on Saturday night.

For fans attending in person, the week promises much more than just competition. Friday's afternoon session features a dressage clinic conducted by a world-class rider, while the Dickies Arena vendor village will offer premium shopping from opening day through Sunday. The Will Rogers Memorial Center campus surrounding the arena provides ample space for pre-show socializing, and Fort Worth's Cultural District — home to world-class museums and acclaimed restaurants — sits right at the arena's doorstep.

DEFENDING CHAMPION

Charlotte Fry & Glamourdale: The Pair to Beat

The World No. 1 combination returns to defend their crown with a flying-themed Freestyle that has captivated crowds across Europe
Rider Profile

Charlotte "Lottie" Fry (GBR), age 29, and her 15-year-old Dutch Warmblood stallion Glamourdale have established themselves as the dominant force in world dressage. Their partnership — built through the Van Olst Horses stable — has produced a World Championship double in Herning (2022), Olympic bronze in Paris (2024), and back-to-back World Cup Final appearances including the title in Basel (2025).

This season, Fry has been in blistering form, winning World Cup qualifiers in London (89.17% Freestyle) and Amsterdam (89.31% Freestyle). She enters Fort Worth as the automatic qualifier and overwhelming favorite, though her fellow British riders and Germany's Isabell Werth will ensure she faces stiff competition. Follow her on Instagram @charlottejfry for behind-the-scenes stable content and competition updates.

LEGEND IN THE SADDLE

Isabell Werth: The G.O.A.T. Chases Another Title

Germany's record-breaking rider brings rising star Wendy de Fontaine to Texas in pursuit of an eighth World Cup crown
Rider Profile

Isabell Werth (GER) is the most decorated dressage rider in history, with seven Olympic medals, multiple World Championship titles, and a record seven World Cup Final victories across a career spanning over three decades. Now competing with the 12-year-old mare Wendy de Fontaine (by Sezuan), Werth posted an 87.58% Freestyle in Amsterdam and sits near the top of the Western European League standings.

Werth made a strategic late-season decision to campaign Wendy instead of her long-time partner DSP Quantaz, and the pair have shown rapid improvement through Frankfurt, Basel, and Amsterdam. At 57, Werth continues to defy time and remains one of the most followed riders in the sport. Follow her on Instagram @isabellwerth_official for training insights and competition day stories.

BRITISH RISING STAR

Becky Moody & Jagerbomb: The Homebreed Sensation

The self-produced pair have quietly risen to the podium at every major qualifier this season
Rider Profile

Becky Moody (GBR) and Jagerbomb represent one of the most compelling underdog stories in current dressage. Jagerbomb (by Dante Weltino OLD) was bred by Moody herself, and together they've climbed from relative obscurity to consistent podium finishes at the highest level, including second place at the 2025 London Freestyle (86.41%) and third at Amsterdam (85.31%).

Moody has confirmed she is targeting the Fort Worth Final if she qualifies, sitting third in the World Cup standings. Her connection with Jagerbomb is beloved by fans for its evident harmony and mutual trust. The pair's rise mirrors the broader strength of British dressage, which has dominated the 2025–2026 qualifying season. Follow Becky on Instagram @beckymoody_dressage for horse life and competition vlogs.

VIVIAN'S INSIDER GUIDE

Getting Around Fort Worth Like a Local

The Horselovers Concierge · Travel Desk
Your Concierge Tips for the Week

Dickies Arena sits in Fort Worth's Cultural District, adjacent to the historic Will Rogers Memorial Center. If you stay at The Crescent Hotel or Hotel Dryce, you can walk to the arena in under 15 minutes — perfect for evening sessions when Fort Worth's April weather is typically warm and pleasant (expect highs around 75–82°F). From downtown hotels like the Omni or the Kimpton Harper, Uber rides to Dickies Arena run about 8 minutes and $8–12.

The Fort Worth Cultural District is where you'll want to spend your non-competition hours. The Kimbell Art Museum, Modern Art Museum, and Amon Carter Museum of American Art are all world-class and within walking distance of the arena. The Fort Worth Stockyards — about a 10-minute Uber north — are absolutely worth a visit for the daily cattle drive, western shopping, and iconic steakhouses. The Stockyards are also where many equestrian industry people congregate socially, so you may spot riders and trainers at Hotel Drover's 97 West Kitchen + Bar or H3 Ranch.

For the best chance of spotting competitors and their entourages, keep your eyes on the Cultural District hotels closest to the arena during the week. Riders and grooms typically stay near the venue or the Will Rogers stabling area. The vendor village inside Dickies Arena will be a major gathering spot — plan to arrive early for sessions to shop brands like CWD, Devoucoux, Ariat, LM Boots, and dozens of premium equestrian retailers. Friday's Dressage Clinic session is especially recommended for a more intimate viewing experience and potential interactions with world-class riders.

Finally, don't miss the West 7th Street corridor connecting the Cultural District to downtown — it's packed with restaurants, bars, and shops, and will likely be buzzing with World Cup energy all week. For a special night out after the Saturday Grand Prix Freestyle, book a late dinner at Emilia's or Saint-Emilion to celebrate the crowning of the new champion.
