What the Market Teaches

Six things the top sellers know

  • Niche beats general. A budget planner for nurses on 3×12 shifts converts 3-5× better than a generic one. Specificity is the advantage.
  • Design matters more than features. One seller built a $280K business by making spreadsheets pink instead of green. Aesthetics drive adoption.
  • Bundles create gravity. A suite of related tools generates 3× more engagement than any single tool. The ecosystem is the product.
  • Seasons drive waves. January resolutions, back-to-school, March Madness, holiday cards — timing to demand cycles is a multiplier.
  • Sharing is distribution. Every group-chat link is organic reach. Social tools — trip planners, brackets, bill splitters — market themselves.
  • Show, don't describe. A 15-second screen recording converts better than any written description. Show the tool working.
Future Architecture

Standalone first.
Connected later.

Every tool works perfectly alone. The long game is a protocol — Doccoin or similar — that lets HTML documents discover each other, share state, and form a network no one owns.

1

Build the library

Dozens of tools under unified design. Each a standalone .html file. No backend, no dependencies.

2

Build recognition

The design language becomes the signal. People recognize the ecosystem before any formal connection exists.

3

Add the protocol

Doccoin or equivalent — a lightweight layer letting documents link, reference, and optionally transact.

4

Let revenue emerge

The brand, library, and network are the asset. Monetization becomes available without being forced.
